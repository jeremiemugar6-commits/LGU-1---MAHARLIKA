🧰 Emergency & Waste Preparedness

Checklists, supply inventory, protocols, and emergency contacts.

🚨 Disaster Preparedness Checklist

  • 72-hour emergency food & water supply per household
  • First-aid kit stocked and accessible
  • Emergency contact list printed and posted
  • Evacuation routes identified and communicated
  • Barangay early warning system tested
  • BDRRMC team roles assigned and briefed
  • Community drill conducted (last 6 months)
  • Backup communication devices charged
  • Senior citizens & PWDs with special needs registered
  • Hazard maps posted at barangay hall

♻️ Waste Preparedness Checklist

  • Segregation bins present in all puroks
  • Collection schedule posted and distributed
  • Crew PPE (gloves, masks, vests) complete
  • Collection vehicle serviceable & fueled
  • Disposal site / MRF coordination confirmed
  • Hazardous waste handling protocol distributed
  • Backup crew roster available
  • Resident IEC materials on waste segregation updated

🏚️ Evacuation Center Readiness

  • Centers identified & capacities confirmed
  • Registration system in place
  • Sleeping mats / cots available
  • Food supply for at least 3 days
  • Drinking water supply confirmed
  • Sanitation facilities operational
  • Focal persons assigned per center
  • Security arrangement in place

🌊 Flood Response Protocol

  1. 1Activate Barangay Disaster Risk Reduction & Management Council (BDRRMC)
  2. 2Broadcast early warning via PA system and group chats
  3. 3Open designated evacuation centers; assign focal persons
  4. 4Coordinate with BFP, PNP, and MDRRMO for rescue assets
  5. 5Deploy rescue teams to flood-prone puroks
  6. 6Log all evacuees; update capacity in system
  7. 7Distribute food packs and water to evacuees
  8. 8Submit situational report to LGU / MDRRMO every 6 hours

🔥 Fire Emergency Protocol

  1. 1Call BFP hotline: Bureau of Fire Protection 160
  2. 2Evacuate affected households immediately
  3. 3Alert neighboring puroks to clear roads for fire trucks
  4. 4Set up command post away from fire area
  5. 5Account for all residents; identify missing persons
  6. 6Open evacuation center for displaced families
  7. 7Coordinate relief goods with DSWD & LGU

♻️ Waste Overflow / Illegal Dumping

  1. 1Receive and log complaint from resident or crew report
  2. 2Dispatch inspection team within 24 hours
  3. 3Document with photos; assess health & environmental risk
  4. 4Issue notice of violation if dumper is identified
  5. 5Mobilize cleanup crew with proper PPE
  6. 6Coordinate with CENRO for hazardous materials
  7. 7Post advisory in the area; conduct IEC

🌀 Typhoon / Storm Surge Protocol

  1. 1Monitor PAGASA bulletins; pre-position response teams at Signal 1
  2. 2Issue mandatory evacuation orders for coastal & low-lying areas at Signal 3+
  3. 3Cut electrical supply in flood-prone areas (coordinate DLPC/MORESCO)
  4. 4Secure community assets; clear drainage canals
  5. 5Deploy 24-hour rapid response team
  6. 6After storm: assess damage; submit RDANA report

🫀 CPR (Adult)

ℹ️
Call 911 first, then begin CPR. Continue until help arrives.
  1. 1Check for responsiveness; tap shoulder and shout "Are you OK?"
  2. 2Call 911 or ask bystander to call; get AED if available
  3. 3Position hands: heel of hand on center of chest (lower half of sternum)
  4. 4Push hard and fast: 100–120 compressions per minute, 5–6 cm deep
  5. 5After 30 compressions: tilt head, lift chin, give 2 rescue breaths (1 sec each)
  6. 6Repeat 30:2 cycle; switch rescuer every 2 minutes to avoid fatigue

🩸 Severe Bleeding

  1. 1Put on gloves or use barrier (plastic bag) to protect yourself
  2. 2Apply firm, direct pressure with clean cloth or bandage
  3. 3Do NOT remove the cloth — add more on top if blood soaks through
  4. 4Elevate the wound above heart level if possible
  5. 5For limb wounds: apply tourniquet 5–8 cm above wound; note time applied
  6. 6Keep patient warm; treat for shock; transport to hospital

🌡️ Heat Stroke

🚨
Life-threatening! Body temp may exceed 40°C. Act immediately.
  1. 1Move victim to cool, shaded area immediately
  2. 2Call 911; heat stroke is a medical emergency
  3. 3Remove excess clothing; fan aggressively
  4. 4Apply cool water / ice packs to neck, armpits, groin
  5. 5Do NOT give fluids if unconscious or vomiting
  6. 6Monitor breathing; be ready to perform CPR

🌊 Near-Drowning

  1. 1Ensure scene is safe; do NOT enter water unless trained
  2. 2Throw rope, life ring, or extend a pole to victim
  3. 3Once on land: check responsiveness and breathing
  4. 4If not breathing: begin CPR (30:2 cycle)
  5. 5Turn victim on side if vomiting occurs
  6. 6Keep warm with blanket; transport to hospital even if conscious

🐍 Snake / Animal Bite

  1. 1Keep victim calm; immobilize bitten limb at heart level
  2. 2Remove jewelry/tight clothing near the bite site
  3. 3Do NOT suck venom, cut the wound, or apply tourniquet
  4. 4Clean wound gently with soap and water if bite is non-venomous
  5. 5Transport immediately to nearest hospital with anti-venom
  6. 6For dog/cat bites: wash wound 15 min with soap; get anti-rabies

⚡ Electric Shock

⚠️
Do NOT touch the victim while they are still in contact with electrical source.
  1. 1Turn off power source at breaker / disconnect plug
  2. 2If unable to cut power: use dry wood/rubber to push victim away
  3. 3Check for responsiveness; call 911
  4. 4If not breathing: begin CPR immediately
  5. 5Cover burns with dry, non-stick bandage
  6. 6Treat for shock; transport to hospital for full evaluation
